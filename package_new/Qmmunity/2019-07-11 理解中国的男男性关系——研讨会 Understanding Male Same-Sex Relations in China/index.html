理解中国的男男性关系——研讨会 Understanding Male Same-Sex Relations in China

Qmmunity
2019年07月11日 07:45

就在上周日（7月7号），虹社与康教授一起给大家带来了一场关于男男伴侣关系的探讨。

Last Sunday, Professor Kang took us through an exploration of the perception of male same-sex relations in China.



中国人到底如何看待男性同性伴侣？如此简单的问题却没有一个直接的答案。对于中国这样一个拥有长时间不间断的历史底蕴的国家来说，人们对于男男性伴侣的认识取决于社会和历史的沉淀，继而进一步加深了这个问题的复杂性。我们从中国古典文学追溯到中国当代的酷儿政治，去探讨不断变化中的这种关系的意义。

How do the Chinese view male same-sex relations? The straight-forward question defies a simple answer. In China, a country of long and uninterrupted history, the meaning of male same-sex relations varies depending on the social and political context of the historical period. Professor Kang’s talk traced this changing meaning from classical Chinese writings to contemporary queer politics.




中国最早期的关于男男性伴侣的记载可以追溯到先秦和汉代，那个时候对于男男性关系的描写是君王与男宠这样不平等的情感关系，这也是“分桃断袖”的由来。对比起之后《后汉书》中邓通的故事，我们可以看出随着时间的变化，男宠的形象在文字书写中更加负面了。

The earliest record of male same-sex relations in China can be traced to the pre-Qin period and Han Dynasty. Such relations during that time were defined as unequal relationships between the king and his male favorites. 


到明代，特别是晚明时期。情教的盛行直接影响到了文人墨客对于同性伴侣看法都是基于情之所至，对于男男性伴侣关系的描写比之前更趋于一种平等的关系。不过随着明朝的衰败，这个时期很快就结束了。

Throughout the Ming Dynasty, especially during its late period, the prevalence of sentimentalism directly affected the perception of same-sex couples. Historical letters described male same-sex relations as equal relationships. However, with the fall of the Ming Dynasty, this positive outlook came to an end.


在清朝，文人和官员供养戏子是一种身份的象征。在《品花宝鉴》一书中，将两人相隔一个舞台，也能交心的情谊描绘为真正的含情脉脉，而那些满脑子只想着性的富家子弟被赋予了一个丑恶的形象。

During the Qing Dynasty, officials began financially supporting actors, the act of which became a status symbol of authority and elegance. 


虽然这样的故事在历史上有没有真实发生过还未可知，不可否认的是当时的确有这样的文学作品流传。

We don’t know whether such stories have actually happened, but the literature describing male same-sex relations throughout history is undeniable.





康教授背景介绍：

Some background information about Professor Kang:


康文庆教授是一位来自克利夫兰州立大学的历史系副教授，毕业于加利福尼亚大学圣克鲁兹分校并获得了历史学博士学位。康教授是美国学术协会理事会研究基金获得者，一直致力于研究中国性别史和性史。

Wenqing Kang is an Associate Professor in the Department of History at Cleveland State University. He received his PhD in History from the University of California, Santa Cruz. He is a recipient of the American Council of Learned Societies fellowship and his research interest is history of gender and sexuality in China.


他的文章《中国的同性恋去罪化与去病理化》收录在Rowman和Littlefield Publishers2012年出版的《China In and Beyond the Headlines》一书中。康教授在2009年通过香港大学出版社出版《癖——20世纪上半期中国的男男性关系》，目前正在研究社会主义时期的男男性关系。

His article “Decriminalization and Depathologization of Homosexuality in China” is included in the book China in and Beyond the Headlines published by Rowman and Littlefield Publishers (2012). His book Obsession: Male Same-Sex Relations in China, 1900-1950 was published by Hong Kong University Press in 2009. He is currently researching homosexuality in the People’s Republic of China.
