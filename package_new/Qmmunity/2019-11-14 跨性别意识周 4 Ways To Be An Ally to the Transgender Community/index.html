跨性别意识周 4 Ways To Be An Ally to the Transgender Community

跨性别意识周

Transgender Awareness Week!



本周（11月13日至19日）是2019年度的跨性别意识周。旨在让众多的跨性别人士和同盟们一起提高社会大众对于该群体的认识：让大众了解到什么是【跨性别】；通过分享个人故事和经历，呼唤社会层面对于跨性别者的支持，一起抵制长久以往对于跨性别群体的偏见、歧视甚至是暴力。一年一度的跨性别意识周是一个全球性的，提升【跨性者（transgender）】、【非性别二元者（non-binary）】和其他非顺性别人群的社会可见度、社会安全、尊严与权力的绝佳时机。跨性别意识周，通常在每年 11月的第二周，然后一直持续到每年的跨性别纪念日（Transgender Day of Remembrance， TDOR）。

Transgender Awareness Week is a week when transgender people and their allies take action to bring attention to the community by educating the public about who transgender people are, sharing stories and experiences, and advancing advocacy around the issues of prejudice, discrimination, and violence that affect the transgender community. Celebrated each year, it is a vital opportunity to advocate for the visibility, protection, dignity, and rights of trans, non-binary and gender-expansive people around the world. Transgender Awareness Week, typically observed in the second week of November, is a one-week celebration leading up to Transgender Day of Remembrance (TDOR). 

 



跨性别纪念日在每年的11月20日，是为了纪念和缅怀每一位在1998年反跨性别暴力事件中去世的跨性别者们而设立的。第一次跨性别纪念日是由跨性别活动家Gwendolyn Ann Smith提出，悼念在1998年被杀害的Rita Hester。之后逐渐由个人层面的吊唁上升到对于所有在当年被杀害的跨性别人士的集体哀悼与追思，最后演变成了现在一年一度的跨性别纪念日。
TDOR occurs annually on November 20, an observance that honors the memory of the transgender people whose lives were lost in acts of anti-transgender violence that year. TDOR was founded by transgender advocate Gwendolyn Ann Smith as a vigil to honor her memory of Rita Hester, a transgender woman who was killed in 1998. The vigil commemorated all the transgender people lost to violence that year and began an important memorial that has become the annual Transgender Day of Remembrance.
 
【跨性别（trans）】是一个用于描述那些【心理性别与天生的生理性别不符，或者对于生理性别感到不适】群体的伞状术语。说起跨性别人群，我们可能会想到很多词语来形容ta们，譬如：跨性别者，变性者，酷儿，男跨女，女跨男；还有一些和ta们密不可分的一些词语，如：流动性别，非性别二元者，异装者，无性人，中性人，第三性别，双性人等等。
The term trans is an umbrella term to describe people whose gender is not the same as, or does not sit comfortably with, the sex they were assigned at birth. Trans people may describe themselves using one or more of a wide variety of terms, including (but not limited to) transgender, transsexual, gender-queer (GQ), gender-fluid, non-binary, gender-variant, crossdresser, genderless, agender, nongender, third gender, bi-gender, trans man, trans woman, transmasculine, transfeminine and neutrois. 

4种方法 4 Ways


为了提高大家的跨性别意识，让大家成为跨性友好人士，以下是一些重要贴士：
To mark Trans Awareness Week, here are some important reminders to know about how to support the transgender community provided by the HRC:

1
Each Person is Unique
1.     当你遇到了一个跨性别人士…嗯，你只是遇到另外一个普通的（跨性）人而已
1. When you meet one transgender person… you’ve only met one transgender person.


每一个跨性别者都有完全不同的经历。虽然我们会把ta们统称为跨性别，但是ta们每一个人的性别认知，性别表达和人生经历都截然不同。和ta们交谈之后就会发现，这些千差万别的过往经历塑造了现在的ta们。
但更重要的是，ta们跟你我都一样，也只是普普通通的一个人而已。
There is not just one sweeping transgender experience. The term “transgender” describes a collective community of individuals whose gender identities, expressions and/or lived experiences differ from what is typically associated with the sex they were assigned at birth. Trans people have different stories and narratives that make them who they are.
2
Respect Boundaries
2.     每一个人都有ta们自己的界限
2. Everyone has different personal boundaries.
 
每一个人的情况都不太一样。所以在得到对方的首肯之前，尽量避免问太过敏感的问题：比如说关于ta的身体，手术，或者跨性的过程等等。其次，一个跨性者跟你聊了某一个私人话题，并不代表ta会跟你聊所有的话题。Again，ta们也是普通人，每一个人都有每个人不同的界限。
Refrain from asking transgender people personal questions about their bodies, surgeries and other aspects about their transition process unless you have their consent to do so. And, just because a trans person might have talked with you about one personal topic doesn’t mean they want to discuss everything. And, remember: Different people have different boundaries.


3
They Determine Their Identifiers 
3.     非性别二元者有可能认为ta是跨性别，是顺性别，也可能两者都不是…
这些都是ok的啦
3. Non-binary people can identify as transgender or cisgender -- or neither -- and that’s OK.


非性别二元论者认为他们不仅仅是传统意义上的【男性】或【女性】，或这两者都不是。Ta们也可能用酷儿，流动性别等词汇来描述ta们的性别认知。最重要的是：不论ta是怎样表述自己的，我们都应该尊重ta们。
Non-binary describes individuals who do not identify exclusively as men or women, or who do not identify as either at all. A non-binary person may also use the terms genderqueer, gender fluid, gender non-conforming or others to describe their gender identity. It’s essential to always respect the terms that someone uses to describe themselves.

4
No "Right" Way to Be Trans
4.     没有一个【正确】的跨性之路
4. There is no “right” way to be trans.


正如中国有56个民族一样，跨性人群也分布在各地，形式多种多样。各式各样的的跨性别者就在我们身边，一同生活，一起学习。或许ta们也想双11在网上血拼买些什么呢。光靠外貌我们很难判断一个人是不是跨性别，更重要的是每一个人都有ta们【真实地做自我】的方式。也不是所有的跨性别者都想要接受手术或者服用荷尔蒙，但是不会因为这一点ta就不是个跨性别人士了。不论如何视而不见，跨性别人士一直都会在这里。
Trans people are everywhere and as diverse as the fabric of a nation. They live and work in every town, study at school, shop for the best deals at the mall -- or hunt for the best avocados in the produce aisle at your grocery store. You can’t tell if someone is trans just by looking at them, and there is no one, universal way that all trans people live their truths. Not all trans people want surgery or hormones, and no one is more or less trans because of it. Trans people have always been here and #WontBeErased.

 


在中国，对于跨性人士有着长久以往的歧视，ta们在社会上四处受限碰壁，以及公众缺乏对于跨性别的认知和教育。几乎没有合规的医疗机构可以提供变性手术，也鲜有开具荷尔蒙的正规专业途径。这些都迫使人们转向黑市或者线上。人们无法在没有家属合法授意的情况下进行变性手术，而且大多数跨性别者根本不敢和家人提及这个问题。否则很可能的结果就是被逐出家门，断绝关系。中国的法律允许跨性别者在性别重置手术后更改ta们身份证件上的性别。对于那些没有办法手术或者无法负担手术的人，ta们只能在这样的现状下生存。Ta们也会因此时常受到公开的羞辱，也无法获得一些基础的公共服务。
In China there is prevalent discrimination, restrictive eligibility requirements and a lack of information regarding transgenderism. There are few medical facilities that offer gender reassignment surgery and little professional information on hormone treatment, forcing people to turn to the black market or online. People are unable to have gender-reassignment surgery without the legal consent of their families, and many are reluctant to discuss the issue with their family – for fear of being ostracised or disowned. Chinese law allows transgender people to change the gender identity on official documents only on condition of Sex Reassignment Surgery (SRS). For those that do not want SRS, or cannot afford to do so, these requirements leave them with identity documents that do not match their gender identity, resulting in frequent public humiliation, and barriers in accessing basic services.


因此，在中国，跨性别意识周就显得更加重要。让我们一起携起手来增加跨性别人士的社会透明度和曝光度，让更多的人了解到ta们，继而减少对于ta们的恶意与歧视。
In China, it is therefore very important to celebrate Transgender Awareness Week in order to increase the visibility of trans people who continue to face discrimination.
