职场上的你，怎么骄傲做彩虹色的自己？LGBTQ @ Work

不知工作环境是否友同，性少数群体如何职场求生？

Best tips for LGBT to survive an uncertain work environment:

到适合自己个性找一个适合自己个性、职业规划的企业机构就业

Find a place that suits you and your career aspirations

每个行业或多或少都有友同的企业机构。友同企业通常会有促进包容性和多元化的措施，以及对歧视零容忍的政策。找工作时，尽量去寻找允许员工做真实自我的企业就业。

While it may be uncommon, every industry will have gay-friendly places. Companies who are LGBTQ friendly will have clear diversity inclusion initiatives and a zero discrimination policy. Seek companies that have policies and practices that help their employees be themselves.

出柜不是必须，谋略不能没有

Coming out is not mandatory, it is a strategic decision

如果你想在职场中出柜，可以考虑出柜。但除非对工作环境非常有把握，最好分阶段进行：先跟比较亲近的同事出柜，观察他们的反应；如果反应良好，则可以更大范围地出柜。性少数群体身处不确定的环境，一定要懂得如何测试同事的接受度。

If you should choose to, coming out can be considered. Unless particularly assuring, most people might consider coming out in stages: first to a few close counterparts, evaluate the response and eventually widen the audience if deemed acceptable. Testing the water is something most minorities know how to do when uncertain.

先试水再下水；性少数群体可以选择适合自己的方式与他人分享个人生活的细节

Test the waters. Being a minority, you can still share your life at the level you feel comfortable

在理想的情况下，一个人的职业发展不应该取决于其个人生活的状况，但实际中却难以避免。不向同事领导出柜，不意味着个人生活完全无法和他们讨论分享。无论直人还是性少数人士，都应该学会如何与同事融洽相处，在职业上取得进步。具体的做法因人而异，但最适合自己的方式，只有通过自主探索才能得出。

In theory your private life should not impact your career progression, but in practice it does carry certain weight. While you may choose not to share your sexual orientation at work, there are still many other aspects of yourself that you could share with your colleagues. Straight or gay, learning to get along and get ahead at work is a skill worth developing. It is a style unique to you, and only you can design one that makes you feel most comfortable in.

明确的表达你希望他人以何种方式与你相处；並做好后备预案

Communicate how you would like others to interact with you, and always have plan B

如果决定在职场出柜，得考虑好如何面对各种典型回应。如果同事没有剧烈反应，相处模式跟往常一样保持不变，那当然最好。但最好提前制定后备预案，万一同事领导难以接受，或负面反应激烈。还有另外一种可能性：同事忽然不知道该怎么和你相处，感到迷茫，就仿佛你是刚来办公室的陌生新人——他们想对你表示友好，但却不知道具体该怎么做。这时候你就该承担起责任，教育同事，并充分把握此过程中的战略机遇，体现出你的自信和包容（要想别人接受你，你也得接受别人）。有些人出柜后和同事更亲密了，和同事一起去美甲、看帅哥…有些人即使出柜了，平时也不愿意跟同事谈起性向。无论你是哪种类型，都应该让同事知道，确保同事尊重你的个人界限。

If you do chose to come out, be prepared for the most common responses. All is well if your colleagues are unfazed and continues to be ‘business as usual’, do have some form of a back up plan should things do not go well or you face severe backlash. There’s also the possibility that others do not know what to do with you. Imagine someone new at work and you’re not sure how to interact with this person with elements that you are unfamiliar with: While your intentions may mean well, it is important to check if this is how it is best to interact. Being a source of guidance and letting others know how to interact with you would be your responsibility, and it would be strategic too. It demonstrates confidence and willingness to be inclusive (acceptance is a two way street). Some might go all out and do mani-padi, shopping and checking out guys together, while others may have a preference that it is never made into a subject of discussion. Whichever way you choose, communicate your preference and have your boundaries respected.

给自己找个职场导师或职业发展教练（或者两个都找）

Get a mentor, coach, or both

职场上，中产阶级白种人男性占绝对优势，其他群体多多少少都会面对某种歧视。在由白人男性主导的企业环境中，许多人感到无所适从。面临不公正的工作环境，有的人会加倍努力工作，竭力当好队友；有的人则是工作态度消极被动，甚至随时可能情绪爆发。找一个适合你的，有资质的职业发展教练，可以很大程度上帮你提升自我认知，弥补性格和职业上的不足。

If you’re not white, male, middle-upper class, chances are you’ll have some chip on your shoulder, or have no idea how to thrive in a white male dominant corporate environment. And individuals each respond to such settings differently: some more productive and collaborative, while others could be downright reactive or hostile to neutral environmental stimuli. An appropriate and qualified mentor or coach can help you raise your self-awareness and develop areas that you may be lacking, both professionally and personally. 


结语 Conclusion 

每个人都是独特的个体，有着独一无二的性格，生活境遇也各不相同。无论是性取向还是其他个人潜质都可能影响日常生活甚至工作。在企业里工作，融入企业文化是必须的，但不能以牺牲真实自我为代价。能够公开诚实地分享个人生点滴当然是件好事，但就算是直人，恐怕也不愿意个人生活的所有方面都毫无保留地展示在他人面前。

Each of us is unique with our individual character, personality and circumstance. Sexualities and other private but salient personal aspects influence our day-to-day lives, even at work. Working in a corporate environment requires that we are better prepared to fit into the organization’s culture without compromising who we truly are. While it is great to be open and honest about all aspects of your life, even for straight individuals very few people want to be read like an open book.
