Every month LQ will have a monthly Professional Mixer at WeWork Weihai Lu. The monthly event will focus on a topic or an industry to bring together the community and allies to share experiences, give advice, and network.

本次

讨论会由LQ的联合创始人之一，王蕾主持进行。

The panel was led by Lei Wang, a co-founder of LesQueers. 


作为一名艺术家或是设计师，你认为你的身份认同给你的职业发展带来了哪些方面的影响？

As an artist & designer, what influences have your identities brought to your careers?

○

Question 1


Diego Wu-Law 和 Ratima Suwanrumpha 分享说，他们多文化的成长背景以及酷儿的身份，培养了他们更多元化的视角，拥有丰富的灵活性，在建筑设计艺术领域，这些对于准确的捕捉客户真正的需求，设计出更有创意的产品有非常大的帮助。

Diego Wu-Law and Ratima Suwanrumpha shared that their multicultural background and queer identities have cultivated their diversified views and flexibility, these are benefits for capturing customers’ needs precisely and designing more creative products.


李博文（Bowen Li）则笑称到，自己虽然是直男，但身边大部分的行业内伙伴和都属于LGBTA+群体。但身份并不重要，我们对于时尚的看法会有不同，但这种不一样正巧是可以发生碰撞，给我带来灵感的。

Bowen Li jokingly expressed that even if he is straight, most of his peers in the design & fashion industry belong to the LGBTQIA+ community. Identity doesn’t matter much; each person possesses different views on fashion which can bring inspiration.



黄愈惠（Jenny Huang）我从来没有把酷儿身份隐藏过、或显示过，它没有在我身上被标签化。我从来没有觉得酷儿身份影响了我做任何事情，甚至这个身份让拥有了更多。我拥有更多元的朋友，更开放的思想，同时，我也比别人承担更多的责任。作为酷儿意味着我们需要变得更专业，对于生活，对于伴侣，更多用一份心。当别人看到我们是这么自信，我们也可以过的这么好的时候，这个标签就不会令其他人对我们的专业进行质疑。这个身份应该是我们的骄傲，而不是我们的累赘。

Jenny Huang shared that she never intentionally hid her queer identity, but she has never intentionally brought attention to it either. Being queer has brought her various benefits such as a more diversified group of friends and an open mindset. Meanwhile, she takes responsibility towards the way she presents herself professionally and personally. As a member of the queer community, we should emphasize our professionalism, and be more devoted to our lives and partners. When others see that we are confident and we can live decent lives, our queer identity will not cause others to question our professionalism. Our identity should be our pride, not our burden.


你希望通过你的作品和你的设计向外界传达什么信息？

What message do you want to be portrayed to the outside world through your design?

○

Question 2


Diego Wu-Law 和 Ratima Suwanrumpha: 作为一个设计者，我们希望人们能通过我们的产品，在空间里切切实实感受到，三种感觉：我很快乐，我很性感以及我很酷。

Diego Wu and Ratima Suwanrumpha: As a designer, we hope that people can gain three kinds of feelings through our products: happiness, sexiness and a sense of cool.


李博文（Bowen Li）： 我想我传递的是融合的概念。在高级定制中，是将定制客人的需求与我自己设计理念的融合。在成衣线中，体现在每个人的多重角色的融合。将工作中与下班后的角色融合起来，并体现你的优雅。

Bowen Li: The concept I want to deliver is fusion. Advanced customization is the fusion of customers’ needs and my design philosophy. Ready-made garments reflect the fusion and multiplicity of people’s roles. Combining your roles during and after work will embody your elegance.


对于同样希望进入艺术和设计领域的年轻一代，你们能分享一个最佳的建议？

Can you share a piece of advice for the young generations who want to enter the art and fashion fields? 

○

Question 3


Diego Wu-Law：只有一个建议吗？那Run Away吧。哈哈哈开个玩笑。

Diego Wu-Law: Just one tip? Run away from it.  I’m just teasing.


Ratima Suwanrumpha：我的建议是你要清楚的知道自己正在做什么。设计这个工作非常艰难，如果没有极度的热爱，这条路对你来说会异常辛苦，并且也很难坚持下来。

Ratima Suwanrumpha: My tip is that you need to be clear about what you are doing right now. Designing is difficult and it is hard to persist through the challenges without great passion.


Diego Wu-Law：我来补充一点积极的讯息，坚持你认定的事情。在设计世界里，所有的努力都不是立竿见影的。你需要拥有一颗坚韧的心，或许数月，或许数年，等待别人看到你的作品，肯定你的价值。

Diego Wu-Law: Let me share a positive message - stick to your determination. In the world of design, success cannot be achieved in the short-term. You need to possess a strong will to wait for others to appreciate and value your work; it may take months or even years.


黄愈惠（Jenny Huang）：不设限，永远保持五岁的好奇心。

Jenny Huang: Don’t set boundaries for yourself, stay curious like a child.


之后

现场参与的观众，也积极的提出了问题。

Later the audience raised some questions.


Jenny 提到要永远保持五岁的好奇心，那如何能保持呢？

Jenny mentioned that we need to be curious, how do you keep your curiosity?

○

Question 1


Jenny: 不管是艺术、行销，广告，大家想收获的都是开心对吧？所以我想问一句，你们有很认真地过你们的生活吗？我曾经一年早餐都吃同一个口味，但很多时候的味道感觉不一样。艺术不代表我要知道这个艺术家是谁，这幅艺术作品有多贵，而是你对艺术的感受。生活就是艺术，今天下了雨，我不会觉得啊好烦，而是觉得：哇，今天下雨了，外面的景有一种浪漫的感觉。每个生活的层面都会给我带来不同的感觉。所以就算我今天是忧伤的，难过的，被老板骂的，失恋状态的，这都为我们的人生留下了不同的风景。只要我们找对了方式，那我们的每一页都会活得很精彩。

Jenny: No matter if its art, marketing, or advertising all people want happiness. I once had the same breakfast for one year. However, the way I felt about the food was different every day. Art doesn’t require knowledge about other artists or financials; your feelings are what matter most. Life is like art; small daily things bring us different feelings. For example, on rainy days, I’ll feel romantic instead of annoyed. No matter what situation we are in, we’ll possess happiness as long as we find our way.


我如今才20出头，但在不停地变换工作，找寻自己到底对什么感兴趣。你们觉得这个状态正常吗？还是说，这个不太对。

I’m in my early twenties, and I keep changing jobs to find out what I’m really interested in. Do you think this is normal?

○

Question 2


Diego Wu-Law：从我个人的经历出发，其实你不停变换工作，不停寻找的过程，是因为就是在成长。比如我在来中国的之前，我始终在做建筑设计，来了之后，我加入了一个公司，后来我又做了老师，再之后我又尝试了一下室内设计，然后我又想说，不如开个公司吧。所有你经历过的，和经历过的历程，最终都将映射在你未来的自己身上。所以大胆去进行不同的尝试，去发现，探索你到底喜欢什么，对什么感兴趣，不要感到害怕。而且特别是在中国，我感觉这里的坏境很包容，你有很多机会去试错，再重新出发。

Diego Wu-Law: From my point of view, the process of changing jobs and discovering is the process of developing. Take my personal experience for example, before I came to China, I did a job related to architecture design. After I came here, I joined a company, and later became a teacher, then tried interior design. Afterwards, I came up with the idea of starting my own business. All the experiences you have accumulated will finally reflect your future. So be bold and try different things to discover what you are interested in and what you are passionate about. Don’t be scared, especially in China, the environment here is inclusive. You have the opportunity to try, fail, and start again.


Lei: 我想在Diego 基础上补充一点，有一句话是说，失败使之进步（Fail Forward), 重要的是你如何看待失败。事实上，每一次失败都使你往前一步，接近成功。

Lei: I want to share some points as well. There’s a saying: fail forward; the important thing is how you view your failure. Every failure will push you forward and closer to success.



李博文（Bowen Li）：我认为，这种顾虑的产生或许是来自中国的传统观念在束缚着。但这个年纪，这个不算困扰。比如我现在30岁，我遇到这种困扰，这可能会成为之后人生道路上的一个坎，但在23岁时遇到这样的事情，应该感到高兴，因为从另一个角度来看，这意味着，摆我面前的机会很多。在年轻的时候所有的尝试都是一种人生经历，并且会在今后跨入另外一个年龄层之后，给你带来很多滋养。

Bowen Li: From my point of view, this kind of worry comes from the constraint of traditional Chinese ideas. However, this is not troublesome at your age. If it occurs when you are over 30, it may become an obstacle for your future. But you should embrace it during your early twenties because from a different perspective it means there are a lot of opportunities lying in front of you. It is necessary to have different experiences when you’re young because it will allow you to grow towards your future development.
