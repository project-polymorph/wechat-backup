社群内歧视

Discrimination in the Community


我们举办了两场讨论会，分别是中文与英文讨论会。两个讨论都探讨了中国LGBTQ社群内所面的重要议题以及西方LGBTQ社群内所面临的有别于中国社群的话题。英语讨论会的内容更为广泛，而中文讨论会主要聚焦于女同与男同关系。我们对两场讨论会进行了录音。这些录音未经编辑或者润色。

We held two different conversations, one in Chinese, and one in English. Both conversations covered important topics that are faced within the Chinese community, and different ones that are being dealt with in the western LGBTQ communities. The English discussion focused on broader topics, whereas the Chinese discussion focused mainly on the specific details within lesbian & gay relationships. We recorded both conversations. These recordings haven’t been edited or polished.

中文版讨论会总结：


首先，我们探讨了相聚在此讨论会的原因。有人说是为了获得讨论、了解LGBT社群所面临问题的机会，获得更深入的认识，并且借此机会寻找解决问题的方法。随后，我们探讨了社群内发生的多种歧视现象与行为，就此分享了相关经历：


歧视的种类：

- 权力的不平等／虐待关系

- 跨性别恐惧症

- 残疾

- 肤色主义

- 种族主义

- 仇外心理/民族主义

- 年龄歧视

- 教育歧视

- 身体歧视

- 财务歧视

- 责备受害者

- 双性恋恐惧症

- 以及其他歧视


歧视可以在以下情境下发生：

- 所有类型的关系

- 身体接触与互动

- 评论

- 互联网互动

- 社群代表

- 活动策划

- 个人偏见

- 以及其他


在充分讨论歧视的发生形式之后，我们就如何防止歧视进行了头脑风暴，并且找到一些措施：

- 达成一致（同意）/让一致性（同意）成为社群文化的一部分

- 愿意从错误中吸取教训

- 举办免费活动

- 反省

- 问责

- 确保每个人都有其代表/占有一席之地

- 以及其他


在中文讨论会上，我们谈了LGBT社群的角色（比如说P和T）以及这些关于“女性化”和“男性化”角色的刻板印象是如何产生害处的。此外，我们还讨论了艾滋病毒状况，以及在社群内对这一疾病的误解。我们的讨论主要集中在人际关系和社区刻板印象上。


我们希望这样的讨论能持续进行，因为讨论会可以让我们的社群环境变得更加安全。欢迎大家加入讨论行列，对自己的行为进行思考，通过实际行动营造一个安全的LGBT社群!






English Discussion: 


In our discussion on Inter-community discrimination, we discussed our reasons for coming to the workshop. Folks said that they wanted to learn, have the change to discuss this issue, gain a greater understanding of different issues within the LGBT community, and learn effective methods for change. We then talked about the many ways that discrimination shows up in our communities, and identified some of the types of discrimination that took place and shared our experiences: 


Types of Discrimination: 

- Consent/Power Violations/Abusive relationships

- Transphobia

- Accessibility

- Colorism

- Racism

- Xenophobia/Nationality

- Ageism

- Education

- Body

- Financial

- Victim Blaming

- Biphobia

- And more!

The ways this discrimination can appear: 

- All types of relationships 

- Physical interactions

- Comments 

- Online

- Who gets represented

- Event planning 

- Personal bias 

- And more


After a thorough discussion about how it manifests, we brainstormed some effective ways to combat this, and practiced how we could respond to various scenarios. Some effective methods that we came up with were:

- Asking for consent/ making consent a part of the culture  

- Being willing to learn from your mistakes

- Hosting sliding scale/free events

- Making events accessible

- Assessing the situation and basing your response on that 

- If online, reporting things to moderators who are trained to educate

- Self-Reflection

- Accountability 

- Making sure that everyone is being represented/has a seat at the table 


And this was just from a couple hours of a few people sitting down together to talk about these issues! This is not the beginning of this conversation, nor is this the end. These are just a few strategies that we came up with for how to step in when you see inter-community discrimination occurring. We hope that these conversations will continue to happen and that folks will reflect on their own behavior and take action to help make our communities safe and welcoming for all members!
